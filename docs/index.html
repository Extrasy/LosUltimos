Vision

Los Últimos Años sees a future where stigma, ageism, ableism, classism, homophobia, racism, sexism, xenophobia, and other forms of bias and discrimination no longer impact access, quality, and outcomes for older adults because everyone deserves to age well and with dignity.

I am an inspiring leader with unwavering passion for community and public service, as demonstrated by 20+ years of experience working with diverse communities and advocating for progress in underserved communities.

About Me

Public Health Professional Certified in Gerontology

Has competent knowledge of public health and healthy aging with an interest in strengthening local, state, and federal support for older adults that include quality of care throughout end stages of life.

Holds a strong passion for educating the public on current and future trends in older adults experiencing Alzheimer’s and related dementias.

Expanding community public health in underserved populations is central to all future goals.

Equipped with a Bachelor’s in Science (B.S.) of Public Health. Special interests and a love for learning procured a Graduate Certificate in Gerontology. Currently a candidate for a Master’s in Public Health- Community Health Education (MPH) from the California State University, Northridge.
